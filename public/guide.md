# How to Play The Flattening

## Goal
Wipe out all opposing civilizations. The last team with population standing wins.

## Controls

| Action | Input |
|--------|-------|
| **Raise terrain** | Left-click |
| **Lower terrain** | Right-click |
| **Move beacon** | Shift + Left-click |
| **Pan camera** | Middle-click drag / Arrow keys / Edge of screen |
| **Zoom** | Mouse wheel |
| **Toggle grid** | G |
| **Inspect unit** | I (then click a walker or settlement) |
| **Cancel** | Escape |
| **Mute music** | M |

## Modes (1-4 keys)

Your walkers obey whichever mode you select:

- **Settle (1)** — Walkers seek flat land and build settlements. Settlements at capacity eject new walkers after 15 seconds.
- **Magnet (2)** — Walkers walk toward the beacon (your flag). Move it with Shift+Click.
- **Fight (3)** — Walkers march toward the nearest enemy walker or settlement.
- **Gather (4)** — Walkers gather near the beacon, useful for amassing forces before switching to Fight.

## Terrain

The world is an isometric island floating in space. Terrain is a grid of height points (0-8).

- **Flat tiles** (all 4 corners equal and above sea level) are where settlements and crops can exist.
- **Raising/lowering** costs 1 mana per click. You can only modify terrain near your own walkers or settlements.
- **Water** tiles have all corners at or below sea level. Walkers drown in water.
- **Rocks** block flatness — no settlements or crops can grow on them.
- **Trees** are cleared automatically by settlements and crop fields.

**Tip:** Flatten land around your settlements to grow more crops and level up faster.

## Settlements

Walkers in Settle mode build settlements on flat tiles. Settlements grow population over time based on surrounding crop fields.

### Levels (1-9)

| Level | Name | Crops Needed | Capacity | Tech |
|-------|------|-------------|----------|------|
| 1 | Tent | 0 | 4 | 0 |
| 2 | Hut | 2 | 8 | 1 |
| 3 | Cottage | 4 | 15 | 1 |
| 4 | House | 7 | 30 | 2 |
| 5 | Large House | 10 | 55 | 2 |
| 6 | Manor | 13 | 90 | 3 |
| 7 | Tower House | 17 | 140 | 3 |
| 8 | Fortress | 20 | 200 | 4 |
| 9 | Castle | 24 | 255 | 4 |

- **Crops** are flat tiles within a 5-tile radius of a settlement (excluding rocks, swamps, ruins, other settlements).
- More crops = higher level = more capacity = higher tech.
- **Castle** (level 9) requires a 5x5 flat area.
- **Tech level** is inherited by ejected walkers and gives a 1.5x combat damage bonus per level of advantage.

### Population Growth
- Each crop field generates 0.1 population per second.
- When at capacity in **Settle mode**, the settlement waits 15 seconds then ejects half its population as a walker.
- In other modes, population caps at capacity with no ejection.

## Walkers

Walkers are your people. They move across the map, fight enemies, and build settlements.

- **Strength** (1-255): Determines combat power. Walkers slowly lose strength over time (attrition).
- **Same-team walkers** that collide merge their strength (max 255) and keep the higher tech.
- **Enemy walkers** that collide fight — the stronger one survives with reduced strength. Tech advantage multiplies effective strength.

### Leaders & the Beacon
- When a walker moves near the beacon and no leader exists, it becomes the **Leader** (shown with a gold crown).
- The Leader is required for the **Knight** power.
- When a Leader dies, the beacon drops at their death location and **locks** — you cannot move it until a new leader is assigned.

## Mana

Mana is generated by your settlement population: 0.0075 per person per second. Maximum 6000. You start with 50.

- Terrain modification costs 1 mana per click.
- Powers cost mana (see below). Spend wisely — mana generation is slow.

## Divine Powers (Hotkeys)

| Power | Cost | Key | Type | Effect |
|-------|------|-----|------|--------|
| **Swamp** | 75 | Q | Targeted | Places 3-5 swamp tiles near the target. Enemy walkers that step on your swamps die instantly. Knights are immune. |
| **Earthquake** | 250 | W | Targeted | Randomly raises and lowers terrain in a large radius around the target, devastating enemy settlements. |
| **Knight** | 400 | E | Auto | Your Leader at a settlement consumes it and becomes a Knight — a powerful fighter with 2x strength, 1.5x speed, and faster attrition. Knights destroy settlements (leaving ruins) and always fight. |
| **Volcano** | 1500 | R | Targeted | Raises terrain to maximum height around the target, adds impassable rocks, and kills everything in the blast radius. |
| **Flood** | 3000 | T | Global | Lowers ALL terrain by 1 height level. Drowns units and destroys settlements on newly submerged tiles. |
| **Armageddon** | All | Y | Global | The endgame. Destroys every settlement, ejecting all population as walkers. Everyone marches to the center of the map for a final battle. No more powers or terrain changes after this. |

**Targeted powers:** Press the hotkey, then click on the map to choose where. Press Escape to cancel.

## Combat Tips

- **Tech matters.** A tech-2 walker deals 1.5x damage against tech-1, and 2.25x against tech-0. Upgrade your settlements.
- **Settle mode** is your economy. More settlements = more mana = more powers.
- **Flatten aggressively.** More flat land = more crops = bigger settlements = stronger walkers.
- **Use swamps defensively** to protect approaches to your settlements.
- **Knights are glass cannons.** Extremely powerful but burn out fast from attrition. Use them to destroy key enemy settlements.
- **Flood** is devastating against low-lying civilizations. Build on high ground.
- **Armageddon** is a gamble — only use it when you have a clear population advantage.

## Game Flow

1. **Early game:** Settle mode. Flatten land, build settlements, grow your economy.
2. **Mid game:** Accumulate mana. Use swamps and knights tactically. Expand territory.
3. **Late game:** Earthquake/Volcano to destroy enemy bases. Flood if they're on low ground.
4. **Endgame:** Armageddon when you have overwhelming numbers, or fight conventionally.

Games typically last 15-25 minutes.
